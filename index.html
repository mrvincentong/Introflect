    A Critical Self-Reflection of Emotional Intelligence
Abstract
Most people begin their day in front of a mirror—shaving, combing hair, brushing teeth, applying cosmetics—they look and see their reflection. But what if the mirror were more than this? What if its function could evolve so that rather than just looking at your outward appearance, you could look within yourself?

Introflect is an experiential installation that explores the phenomena of mirror self-misidentification; framed as a narrative, the project endeavors to help viewers understand empathy in a different context. Imagine a mirror as a window in which you can see two versions of yourself: the reflection you always see, along with the view a third-person perspective.

In the current selfie-filled culture, people often craft an image that does not reveal their true identity. Introflect aims to change this, allowing users to view themselves in a new light, and perhaps, through introspection, see themselves more deeply.

Background

Who Am I?

I'm Vincent Ong, a user experience designer born and raised in Los Angeles. I'm excited about creating experiences that blend creativity and technology in an innovative way to alleviate our daily problems.

Introflect is my Honors Senior Thesis project for the Media Arts + Practice program at the USC. It combines my passion to understand the human mind with my passions of technology and design. My goal is to create an experience that moves people to see themselves in a new light.
